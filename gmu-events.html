Game Jams

Game Jams are a key cornerstone event type here at GMU, one where we providing a platform for students to their game development skills! In these action-packed, fast-paced events, students aim to develop a game in a short amount of time (usually a few days in our case) from start-to-finish!

We host both virtual and in-person Game Jams, so you can choose which environment works best for you. Some prefer the remote workflow while others thrive in a studio setting. Explore the Game Jam events we've hosted in the past to learn more about the experiences, opportunities and final products of them, ready-to-play!

"Clocking Out" by John, Jonathan, Daniel and Shaheed

Tired after a long shift at work, you rest your head in the break room while waiting for the bus. When you wake up, you find out that you've overslept: the store is now closed, and you are "alone"...

Surely it's as simple as walking out the main entrance, right? If this isn't the case, you may need to find a second way out of the store. But, there's no reason you'd need to do this, and there's certainly no reason the manager would still be in the store with you...

Screenshots
"The Lot" by Tristan, Adam, Sam, Sophie, Isaac, and Sander

'The Lot' is an atmospheric walking simulator set in an eerie underground parking lot. The game is a subtle take on horror and is deeply inspired by the concept of liminality. It takes place in an infinite loop, and as such there is no finite ending. Each level of the parking lot is slightly different to the last.
