Game Jams

Game Jams are a key cornerstone event type here at GMU, one where we providing a platform for students to their game development skills! In these action-packed, fast-paced events, students aim to develop a game in a short amount of time (usually a few days in our case) from start-to-finish!

We host both virtual and in-person Game Jams, so you can choose which environment works best for you. Some prefer the remote workflow while others thrive in a studio setting. Explore the Game Jam events we've hosted in the past to learn more about the experiences, opportunities and final products of them, ready-to-play!

Honourable Entries
"EvoCavern" - A Starry Sprites Production

In this 2D roguelike shooter, you must evolve to adapt to your surroundings. Navigate the cave and outsmart flying unibats that will target you from the moment you spawn. In order to level up to get stronger, you must kill them to gain experience. When you level up, items are dropped that can increase your stats. While your health is replenished each round, unibat damage scales with each one killed.

Will you evolve to survive, or become a victim of the caverns?

"How NOT to Train Your Dragon" by [NAME HERE]

A virtual pet simulator where you are challenged to grow Goober (a dragon) while trying not to kill it (optional). You need to earn coins in order to buy food (Goober gets reallly hungry) and other supplementary items to maintain good balance of three main attributes of Goober - health, hunger and fun. As you can see, earning coins is the most important thing for the Goober to stay alive and fully functionable. Minigames and gambling (the most favorite thing for Goober) are going to be the main source of money. But beware, playing minigames is going to affect health of Goober negatievely, so you need to balance playing minigames with other activities. The primary goal of the player is to unlock all of the endings.

"The Lot" by Tristan, Adam, Sam, Sophie, Isaac, and Sander

'The Lot' is an atmospheric walking simulator set in an eerie underground parking lot. The game is a subtle take on horror and is deeply inspired by the concept of liminality. It takes place in an infinite loop, and as such there is no finite ending. Each level of the parking lot is slightly different to the last.
